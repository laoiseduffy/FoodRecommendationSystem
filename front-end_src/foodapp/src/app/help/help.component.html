Pre Exercise Recipes

Meaning: Recipes that will properly fuel your body for intense exercise or a game. These recipes are: low in fibre so as to be easily digested, high in carbohydrates for energy, and low in protein. It is recommended that you eat 2+ hours before exercise to avoid stomach discomfort.

Navigation: To find these recipes go to the home page then click on the recipes link within the Pre-Exercise Recipes card

Post Exercise Recipes

Meaning: These recipes are high in carbs and protein to ensure that you are giving your body the food it needs to repair and refuel. Aim to eat something within an hour of exercise to start the recovery process asap.

Navigation: To find these recipes go to the home page then click on the recipes link within the Post Exercise Recipes card

Recovery Recipes

Meaning:Recipes for the day after exercise that replace fuel stores, repair damage muscle tissue, and reduce inflammation. It should be high in protein, high in fibre and also be nutrient rich. This means you should focus on getting plenty of fruit and vegetables into your meals.

Navigation: To find these recipes go to the home page then click on the recipes link within the Recovery Day Recipes card

Everyday Healthy Recipes

Meaning:These are recipes that promote a healthy lifestyle. They are balanced meals meaning that they include carbohydrates, protein and fats.

Navigation: To find these recipes go to the home page then click on the recipes link within the Everyday Healthy Recipes card
